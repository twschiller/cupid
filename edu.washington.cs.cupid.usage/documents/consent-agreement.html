Cupid Data Collection Terms of Use

This document describes how the Cupid development team at the University of Washington ("Cupid Team") will treat the data provided by individuals and organizations through the Cupid Data Collector.

  • A unique id is generated for every workstation (or Eclipse workspace) that submits data using the Cupid Data Collector. This session id is transmitted every time data is sent to the Cupid Team.
  • The Cupid Team does not intend for the Cupid Data Collector to collect information that can uniquely identify you as an individual. However, it may inadvertently collect information that is included in plug-in metadata, which information may identify an individual or organization.
  • The data that is being collected relates to how you are using Cupid and Eclipse. This includes, but is not limited to, length of time spent in a specific editor/view or perspective, capability invocation data, hashed qualified type names, plugin version numbers, wizard and view session data, etc.
  • The Cupid Data Collector may collect and transmit the country where you are located.
  • The Cupid Team will analyze data collected with the Cupid Data Collector for research purposes.
  • The Cupid Team will publish summary reports based on the data obtained. These reports will be made available in machine readable format that will allow individuals and organizations to undertake further analysis.

Interactions with the Cupid Data Collector

  • With your permission, the Cupid Data Collector will periodically transmit your usage data to the Cupid development team at the University of Washington. The first time it attempts to transmit the data, you will be requested to grant permission of transmission. If you decline the request, the Cupid Data Collector will stop functioning, until you decide to re-enable it. If you grant permission, you will have the option of allowing the Cupid Data Collector to automatically transmit data without further interaction or to request your permission each time it attempts to transmit data.
  • At anytime, you may view the data that is being collected. To view your current information, go to the "Cupid Data Collection | Preview" preferences page.
  • After providing your initial permission, you may discontinue use of the Usage Monitor by turning off the "Enable Capture" checkbox on the "Cupid Data Collection" preferences page.

More Information If you have any questions or would like additional information about the Cupid Data Collector, please contact tws@cs.washington.edu.
